## OctoAcme Project Management Overview

**Project Lifecycle & Core Principles**

OctoAcme follows a structured five-phase project lifecycle: Initiation, Planning, Execution, Release, and Close & Retrospective. The organization is guided by customer-first principles, iterative delivery of small testable increments, clear ownership structures, data-informed decision-making, and a culture of psychological safety. Each project is led by a Project Manager (PM) who coordinates delivery and schedule, paired with a Product Manager (PdM) who defines outcomes and measures success. This dual-leadership model ensures both execution excellence and strategic alignment with business objectives.

**Workflows, Backlogs & Quality Standards**

Work flows through a GitHub Projects-based board with columns spanning Backlog, Ready, In Progress, In Review, QA, and Done. The team maintains a prioritized backlog with clear acceptance criteria, Definition of Done standards, and T-shirt sizing or story point estimates. Pull requests are kept small (≤400 lines when possible) and require at least one approval before merging. Quality is enforced through unit tests for new logic, integration tests where applicable, end-to-end smoke tests for critical flows, and security scanning in CI. The team conducts daily standups (15 minutes), weekly delivery syncs, and demo/review sessions at sprint or milestone completion.

**Communication, Risk Management & Continuous Improvement**

Stakeholder communication is structured through a weekly PM-PdM sync, twice-weekly team standups, and monthly stakeholder updates. Risks are managed via a Risk Register tracking ID, description, impact, likelihood, owner, and mitigation plans—reviewed weekly during syncs. The organization has a clear escalation path: team-level → PM → Product Lead → Sponsor. After each sprint, release, or milestone, the team conducts a 45–75 minute retrospective to capture learnings and convert them into actionable improvements tracked in the project backlog. This commitment to reflection and continuous iteration enables the team to reduce cycle time, maintain transparency, and sustainably improve their delivery practices.

## At-a-glance: OctoAcme Project Management Processes

- **Iterative delivery:** Work is scoped into increments, tested, and shipped frequently.
- **Project lifecycles:** Initiate, plan, execute, release, and improve — each with its own templates and checklists.
- **Clear roles & responsibilities:** See roles/personas doc for PM, PdM, Developer, and Stakeholder expectations.
- **Quality focus:** Testing, acceptance criteria, and review are built-in.
- **Transparency:** Risks, comms, and decisions are documented.
